for it to hold thatopinion., (4)The conditions are—, (a)that the treatment, or non-compliance with the duty, is necessary in ordernot to endanger the health or safety of any person (which may include that ofthe disabled person);, (b)that the disabled person is incapable of entering into an enforceableagreement, or of giving an informed consent, and for that reason thetreatment, or non-compliance with the duty, is reasonable in the particularcase;, (c)that, in the case of treatment mentioned in subsection (1), treating thedisabled person equally favourably would in the particular case
